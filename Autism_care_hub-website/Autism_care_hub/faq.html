Frequently Asked Questions About Autism

1. What is Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)?

ASD is a neurological and developmental disorder that affects how people interact, communicate, learn, and behave. [Source: WHO]

2. What are common signs of autism in children?

Common signs include delayed speech, lack of eye contact, repetitive behaviors, and difficulty understanding social cues. [Source: CDC]

3. At what age can autism be diagnosed?

Autism can often be reliably diagnosed by age 2, though signs may appear as early as 12–18 months. [Source: American Academy of Pediatrics]

4. Is there a cure for autism?

There is no cure for autism, but early intervention and therapies can help improve functioning and quality of life. [Source: WHO]

5. What causes autism?

The exact cause is unknown, but genetics and environmental factors play a role. Vaccines do not cause autism. [Source: CDC]

6. Can people with autism lead independent lives?

Yes, with appropriate support, many individuals with autism live independently and lead fulfilling lives. [Source: Autism Speaks]

7. How is autism diagnosed?

Diagnosis typically involves developmental screenings, behavioral evaluations, and input from specialists like pediatricians and psychologists. [Source: WHO]

8. What therapies help children with autism?

Common therapies include speech therapy, behavioral therapy (ABA), occupational therapy, and social skills training. [Source: WHO]

9. How can I support a family member with autism?

Be patient, learn about their needs, create routines, and seek professional guidance. Support groups can also help. [Source: UNICEF]

10. What should I do if I suspect my child has autism?

Schedule an evaluation with your pediatrician, who may refer you to a developmental specialist for further testing. [Source: WHO, AAP]
